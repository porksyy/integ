Bachelor of Science in
Entertainment and Multimedia Computing (BSEMC)

Introduction


A Bachelor of Science in Entertainment and Multimedia Computing program is an interdisciplinary degree that combines elements of computer science, multimedia design, and entertainment technology. This program is designed to equip students with the knowledge and skills required to create interactive multimedia content for various entertainment platforms, such as games, films, animation, virtual reality, and augmented reality.

The Bachelor of Science in Entertainment and Multimedia Computing program aims to provide students with the necessary creative and technical knowledge, skills, and values to excel as professionals. The program focuses on developing students' abilities to conceptualize, design, and produce animation products and solutions, while also equipping them with project management skills across various technology platforms.

Throughout the program, students will be expose to a wide range of subjects, including computer graphics, game development, interactive storytelling, visual effects, user interface design, audio production, digital animation, and multimedia programming. The program typically includes a combination of theoretical coursework, hands-on projects, and industry internships. Students have the opportunity to work with industry-standard software and hardware tools, collaborating in teams to design and create multimedia projects.

Overall, this program offers a comprehensive blend of technical skills, creative thinking, and industry knowledge, positioning graduates to thrive in the ever-evolving world of entertainment and multimedia computing.
