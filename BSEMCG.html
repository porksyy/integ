About BSEMCG

The Bachelor of Science in Entertainment and Multimedia Computing with Specialization in Game Development (BSEMCG) is a specialized undergraduate degree program that focuses on the field of game development within the broader scope of entertainment and multimedia computing.

Students pursuing a BSEMCG degree gain knowledge and skills related to various aspects of game development, including game design, programming, graphics, animation, audio, and user interface development. The program combines technical and creative elements to prepare graduates for careers in the game development industry.

The curriculum of a typical BSEMCG program includes introductory courses on game development, covering topics such as game design principles, storytelling, game mechanics, and programming languages commonly used in game development like C++, C#, or Java. Students also study computer graphics and animation, learning about 3D modeling, animation techniques, and rendering.
